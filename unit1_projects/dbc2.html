the villanelle

Free-association and free verse style poetry are the dominant preferences, but it's a good exercise to try to write in form sometimes. The rigor can be strangely liberating and can strengthen a sense of rhythm and the skill of rhyme. Form can guide content. What could be greater than literature that uses math? Here are the basics of writing a villanelle.

  • 19 lines long
  • 5 tercets (3-line stanzas) and a final quatrain (4-line stanza)
  • 2 end-rhymes repeat throughout (see a and b)
  • 1st and 3rd lines of the first stanza (see A1 and A2) repeat alternatley as the last line of each subsequent stanza
  • In the final quatrain, A1 and A2 conclude the poem
